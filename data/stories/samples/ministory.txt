The old lighthouse keeper climbed the spiral stairs one last time. For forty years he had guarded this coast, and tonight the automated beacon would replace him. He remembered the storm of 1962, when he pulled three sailors from the black water while the wind screamed. His hands trembled now, not from fear but from grief. At the top he lit the lamp by hand, as he always had. Below, the town lights flickered like distant stars. "Goodbye, old friend," he whispered to the sea. Then he descended, and did not look back.
